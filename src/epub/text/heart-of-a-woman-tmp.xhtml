The Heart of a Woman

v#The heart of a woman goes forth with the dawn, v#As a lone bird, soft winging, so restlessly on, v#Afar o'er life's turrets and vales does it roam v#In the wake of those echoes the heart calls home.

v#The heart of a woman falls back with the night, v#And enters some alien cage in its plight, v#And tries to forget it has dreamed of the stars v#While it breaks, breaks, breaks on the sheltering bars.

The Dreams of the Dreamer

v#The songs of the singer v##Are tones that repeat v#The cry of the heart v##'Till it ceases to beat.

Gossamer

v#The early dew upon the grass, v##The purity of morn, v#The glint that lies in virgin cheek, v##Frail cobwebs—of the dawn.

Sympathy

v#My joy leaps with your ecstasy, v##In sympathy divine; v#The smiles that wreathe upon your lips. v##Find sentinels on mine:

v#Your lightest sigh I'm echoing, v##I tremble with your pain, v#And all your tears are falling v##In my heart like bitter rain.

Contemplation

v#We stand mute! v#No words can paint such fragile imagery, v#Those prismic gossamers that roll v#Beyond the sky-line of the soul; v#We stand mute!

Dead Leaves

v#Again I thread the mazy past, v#Back where the mounds are scattered fast— v#Oh! foolish tears, why do you start, v#To break of dead leaves in the heart?

Dawn

v#Trailing night's sand-sifted stars, v#Rainbows sweep, as day unbars, v#Fragrant essences of morn, v#Bathe humanity—new-born!

Elevation

v#There are highways in the soul, v##Heights like pyramids that rise v##Far beyond earth-veilèd eyes, v##Sweeping through the barless skies v##O'er the line where daylight dies— v#There are highways in the soul!

Peace

v#I rest me deep within the wood, v##Drawn by its silent call, v#Far from the throbbing crowd of men v##On nature's breast I fall.

v#My couch is sweet with blossoms fair, v##A bed of fragrant dreams, v#And soft upon my ear there falls v##The lullaby of streams.

v#The tumult of my heart is stilled, v##Within this sheltered spot, v#Deep in the bosom of the wood, v##Forgetting, and—forgot!

Whither?

v#Minutes swiftly throb and pass, v##Shadows cross the dial-glass, v#Speeding ever to some call, v##Weary world and shadows, all.

v#Down the closing aisles of day, v##Tramping footsteps die away, v#But no tidings thread the gloom, v##From the hushed and silent tomb.

Quest

v#The phantom happiness I sought v##O'er every crag and moor; v#I paused at every postern gate, v##And knocked at every door;

v#In vain I searched the land and sea, v##E'en to the inmost core, v#The curtains of eternal night v##Descend—my search is o'er.

Mate

v#Our separate winding ways we trod, v#Along the highways, unto God, v#Unbonded by the clasp of hand, v#Without a vow—we understand, v#Estranged for aye, the fusing kiss, v#Omnipotent, we bide in this— v#They need no trammeling of bars v#Whose souls were welded with the stars.

Emblems

v#A wordless kiss, a stifled sigh, v#A trembling lip, a downcast eye, v###"Alas," they say, v###"A-day, a-day," v#The cruse has failed, the lamp must die!

Mirrored

v#When lone and solitaire within your chamber, v##With lamp unlit, as evening shades unroll, v#If you reveal the trail your thoughts are taking, v##I then may read the riddle of your soul.

v#For it is then, the tired mind unveiling, v##Drifts stark into the holy after-glow, v#Within the hour of quiet meditation, v##The tidal thoughts, like limpid waters, flow.

Repulse

v#Nobody cares when I am glad, v##I beat upon their hearts in glee, v##"Drink, drink joy's brimming cup with me," v##All echoless, my ecstasy— v#Nobody cares when I am glad.

v#Nobody cares when I am sad, v##Whene'er I seek compassion's breast, v##I falter wounded from my quest v##Back! back into my heart, sore prest— v#Nobody cares when I am sad.

Query

v#Is she the sage who will not sip v#The cup love presses to her lip? v#Or she who drinks the mad cup dry, v#And turns with smiling face—to die?

Pent

v#The rain is falling steadily v##Upon the thirsty earth, v#While dry-eyed, I remain, and calm v##Amid my own heart's dearth.

v#Break! break!! ye flood-gates of my tears v##All pent in agony, v#Rain, rain! upon my scorching soul v##And flood it as the sea!!

Pages from Life

v#The tender eyes have lost their glow, v#The flagons of the lips run low, v#The autumn trembles in the air,— v#A woman passes solitaire!

Recall

v#Winter—aback sweeps the inward eye, v#Fleet o'er the trail to a rose-wreathed sky, v#Girt by a cordon of dreams I dwell v#Deep in the heart of the old-time spell.

v#Almost, the tones of your whispered word, v#Almost! the thrill that your dear lips stirred, v#Almost!! that wild pulsing throb again— v#Almost!!!— v#('Tis winter, the falling rain).

Gethsemane

Impelled

v#Throughout the night's long brooding deep, v#Earth's trustful children die-to-sleep, v#But with the whisperings of morn v#Awake, unto the day, new-born.

v#The mystery of earth untold, v#The great infinite, none behold, v#Forge ever new the spiral chain, v#Revolving man to God again.

Eventide

v#The silence of the brooding night, v#Enfolds me with its eerie light; v#I lie upon its shadowed breast v#A pilgrim, wearying for rest

v#Nightfall! thy sable curtains steep v#My very soul in solace deep, v#God sends thee with thy soothing balms, v#That I may falter to thy arms.

Thrall

v#Fragile, tiny, just a sprite, v#Holding me a thrall bedight, v#Stronger than a giant's wand v#Serves the word of your command.

v#Out from rushing worlds, though low v#Should you whisper, I would know, v#And would answer, though the breath v#Be the gateway unto death.

Youth

v#The dew is on the grasses, dear, v##The blush is on the rose, v#And swift across our dial-youth, v##A shifting shadow goes.

v#The primrose moments, lush with bliss, v#Exhale and fade away, v#Life may renew the Autumn time, v#But nevermore the May!

Joy

v#There's a soft rosy glow o’er the whole world to-day, v#There's a freshness and fragrance that trembles in May, v#There's a lilt in the music that vibrates and thrills v#From the uttermost glades to the tops of the hills.

v#Oh! I am so happy, my heart is so light, v#The shades and the shadows have vanished from sight, v#This wild pulsing gladness throbs like a sweet pain— v#O soul of me, drink, ere night falleth again!

Posthumous

v#Of what avail the tardy showers, v#To the famished summer flowers? v#All in vain the rain-drops cry, v#Dead things never make reply.

v#Life's belated cup of bliss, v#Woo the weary lips to kiss, v#When the singing is a sigh, v#Pulses quivering, to die.

Omega

v#The fragile fabric of our dream v#Drifts as a feather down life's stream— v#The long defile of empty days v#Grim silhouetted, mock my gaze.

v#Though oft escapes the stifled sigh, v#A desert ever broods my eye— v#Since you have utterly forgot, v#God grant that I remember not!

Tears and Kisses

v#There are tears sweet, refreshing like dewdrops that rise, v#There are tears far too deep for the lakes of the eyes.

v#There are kisses like thistledown, fitfully sped, v#There are kisses that live in the hearts of the dead.

Isolation

v#Alone! yes, evermore alone—isolate each his way, v#Though hand is echoing to hand vain sophistries of clay. v#Within that veilèd, mystic place where bides the inmost soul, v#No twain shall pass while tides shall wax, nor changing seasons roll.

v#Enisled, apart our pilgrimage, despite the arms that twine, v#Despite the fusing kiss that wields the magic charm of wine, v#Despite the interplay of sigh, the surge of sympathy, v#We tread in solitude remote, the trail of destiny!

Where

v#No answer echoed to my call, v##And now my way I thread v#About the lowly mounds that rise v##Among the silent dead.

v#Though voiceless, you will hear my call, v##Your soul will heed my cry, v#Will rise, and mock the prison where v##Your bones recumbent lie.

Tired

v#I'm tired, there are none to care v#That I am drifting to despair: v#O shadows! take me to your breast v#For I am tired—I would rest.

Sonnet to the Mantled

v#And they shall rise and cast their mantles by, v#Erect and strong and visioned, in the day v#That rings the knell of Curfew o'er the sway v#Of prejudice—who reels with mortal cry v#To lift no more her leprous, blinded eye, v#Reft of the fetters, far more cursed than they v#Which held dominion o'er human clay. v#The spirit soars aloft where rainbows lie.

v#Like joyful exiles swift returning home— v#The rhythmic chanson of their eager feet, v#While voices strange to ecstasy, long dumb, v#Break forth in major rhapsodies, full sweet. v#Into the very star-shine, lo! they come v#Wearing the bays of victory complete!

Sonnet to Those Who See But Darkly

v#Their gaze uplifting from shoals of despair v#Like phantoms groping enswathed from the light v#Up from miasmic depths, children of night, v#Surge to the piping of Hope's dulcet lay, v#Souled like the lily, whose splendors declare v#God's mazèd paradox—purged of all blight, v#Out from the quagmire, unsullied and fair.

v#Life holds her arms o'er the festering way, v#Smiles, as their faith-sandalled rushes prevail, v#Slowly the sun rides the marge of the day, v#Wine to the lips sorely anguished and pale; v#On, ever on, do the serried ranks sway v#Charging the ultimate, rending the veil.

Brotherhood

v#Come, brothers all! v#Shall we not wend v#The blind-way of our prison-world v#By sympathy entwined? v#Shall we not make v#The bleak way for each other's sake v#Less rugged and unkind? v#O let each throbbing heart repeat v#The faint note of another's beat v#To lift a chanson for the feet v#That stumble down life's checkered street.

Let Me Not Lose My Dream

v#Let me not lose my dream, e’en though I scan the veil with eyes unseeing through their glaze of tears, v#Let me not falter, though the rungs of fortune perish as I fare above the tumult, praying purer air, v#Let me not lose the vision, gird me, Powers that toss the worlds, I pray! v#Hold me, and guard, lest anguish tear my dreams away!

Let Me Not Hate

v#Let me not hate, although the bruising world decries my peace, v#Gives me no quarter, hounds me while I sleep; v#Would snuff the candles of my soul and sear my inmost dreamings.

v#Let me not hate, though girt by vipers, green and hissing through the dark; v#I fain must love. God help me keep the altar-gleams that flicker wearily, anon, v#On down the world’s grim night!

Calling Dreams

v#The right to make my dreams come true v#I ask, nay, I demand of life, v#Nor shall fate’s deadly contraband v#Impede my steps, nor countermand.

v#Too long my heart against the ground v#Has beat the dusty years around, v#And now, at length, I rise, I wake! v#And stride into the morning-break!

Desire

v#Ope! ye everlasting doors, unto my soul’s demand, v#I would go forward, fare beyond these dusty boulevards, v#Faint lights and fair allure me all insistently v#And I must stand within the halls resplendent, of my dreams.

Sorrow Singers

v#Hear their viol-voices ringing v##Down the corridor of years, v#As they lift their twilight faces v##Through a mist of falling tears!

The Cross

v#All day the world’s mad mocking strife, v#The venomed prick of probing knife, v#The baleful, subtle leer of scorn v#That rims the world from morn to morn, v#While reptile-visions writhe and creep v#Into the very arms of sleep v#To quench the fitful burnished gleams: v#A crucifixion in my dreams!

Prejudice

v#These fell miasmic rings of mist, with ghoulish menace bound, v#Like noose-horizons tightening my little world around, v#They still the soaring will to wing, to dance, to speed away, v#And fling the soul insurgent back into its shell of clay:

v#Beneath incrusted silences, a seething Etna lies, v#The fire of whose furnaces may sleep—but never dies!

Laocoön

v#This spirit-choking atmosphere v##With deadly serpent-coil v#Entwines my soaring-upwardness v##And chains me to the soil, v#Where’er I seek with eager stride v##To gain yon gleaming height, v#These noisesome fetters coil aloft v##And snare my buoyant flight.

v#O, why these aspirations bold, v##These rigours of desire, v#That surge within so ceaselessly v##Like living tongues of fire? v#And why these glowing forms of hope v##That scintillate and shine, v#If naught of all that burnished dream v##Can evermore be mine?

v#It cannot be, fate does not mock, v##And man’s untoward decree v#Shall not forever thus confine v##My life’s entirety, v#My every fibre fierce rebels v##Against this servile role, v#And all my being broods to break v##This death-grip from my soul!

Moods

v#My heart is pregnant with a great despair v#With much beholding of my people’s care, v#’Mid blinded prejudice and nurtured wrong, v#Exhaling wantonly the days along: v#I mark Faith’s fragile craft of cheering light v#Tossing imperiled on the sea of night, v#And then, enanguished, comes my heart’s low cry, v#“God, God! I crave to learn the reason why!” v#Again, in spirit loftily I soar v#With winged vision through earth’s outer door, v#In such an hour, it is mine to see, v#In frowning fortune smiling destiny!

Hegira

v#Oh, black man, why do you northward roam, and leave all the farm lands bare? v#Is your house not warm, tightly thatched from storm, and a larder replete your share? v#And have you not schools, fit with books and tools the steps of your young to guide? v#Then what do you seek, in the north cold and bleak, ’mid the whirl of its teeming tide?

v#I have toiled in your cornfields, and parched in the sun, I have bowed ’neath your load of care, v#I have patiently garnered your bright golden grain, in season of storm and fair, v#With a smile I have answered your glowering gloom, while my wounded heart quivering bled, v#Trailing mute in your wake, as your rosy dawn breaks, while I curtain the mound of my dead.

v#Though my children are taught in the schools you have wrought, they are blind to the sheen of the sky, v#For the brand of your hand, casts a pall o’er the land, that enshadows the gleam of the eye, v#My sons, deftly sapped of the brawn-hood of man, self-rejected and impotent stand, v#My daughters, unhaloed, unhonored, undone, feed the lust of a dominant land.

v#I would not remember, yet could not forget, how the hearts beating true to your own, v#You’ve tortured, and wounded, and filtered their blood ’till a budding Hegira has blown.

v#Unstrange is the pathway to Calvary’s hill, which I wend in my dumb agony, v#Up its perilous height, in the pale morning light, to dissever my own from the tree.

v#And so I’m away, where the sky-line of day sets the arch of its rainbow afar,
v#To the land of the north, where the symbol of worth sets the broad gates of combat ajar!

The Passing of the Ex-Slave

v#Swift melting into yesterday, v#The tortured hordes of ebon-clay; v#No more is heard the plaintive strain, v#The rhythmic chaunting of their pain.

v#Their mounded bodies dimly rise v#To fill the gulf of sacrifice, v#And o’er their silent hearts below v#The mantled millions softly go.

v#Some few remaining still abide, v#Gnarled sentinels of time and tide, v#Now mellowed by a chastened glow v#Which lighter hearts will never know.

v#Winding into the silent way, v#Spent with the travail of the day, v#So royal in their humble might v#These uncrowned Pilgrims of the Night!

The Octoroon

v#One drop of midnight in the dawn of life’s pulsating stream v#Marks her an alien from her kind, a shade amid its gleam; v#Forevermore her step she bends insular, strange, apart— v#And none can read the riddle of her wildly warring heart.

v#The stormy current of her blood beats like a mighty sea v#Against the man-wrought iron bars of her captivity. v#For refuge, succor, peace and rest, she seeks that humble fold v#Whose every breath is kindliness, whose hearts are purest gold.

Aliens

(To You—Everywhere! Dedicated)

v#They seem to smile as others smile, the masquerader’s art v#Conceals them, while, in verity, they’re eating out their heart, v#Betwixt the two contending stones of crass humanity v#They lie, the fretted fabric of a dual dynasty.

v#A single drop, a sable strain debars them from their own— v#The others—fold them furtively, but God! they are alone, v#Blown by the fickle winds of fate far from the traveled mart v#To die, when they have quite consumed the morsel of their heart.

v#When man shall lift his lowered eyes to meet the moon of truth, v#Shall break the shallow shell of pride and wax in ways of ruth, v#He cannot hate, for love shall reign untrammelled in the soul, v#While peace shall spread a rainbow o’er the earth from pole to pole.

Concord

v#Nor shall I in sorrow repine, v#But offer a paean of praise v#To the infinite God of my days v#Who marshals the pivoting spheres v#Through the intricate maze of the years, v#Who loosens the luminous flood v#That lightens the purlieus of men, v#I shall not in sorrow repine v#To break the eternal Amen!

The Mother

v#The mother soothes her mantled child v#With incantation sad and wild; v#A deep compassion brims her eye v#And stills upon her lips, the sigh.

v#Her thoughts are leaping down the years, v#O’er branding bars, through seething tears, v#Her heart is sandaling his feet v#Adown the world’s corroding street.

v#Then, with a start she dons a smile v#His tender yearnings to beguile, v#And only God will ever know v#The wordless measure of her woe.

Maternity

v#Proud? v#Perhaps—and yet v#I cannot say with surety v#That I am happy thus to be v#Responsible for this young life’s embarking. v#Is he not thrall to prevalent conditions? v#Does not the day loom dark apace v#To weave its cordon of disgrace v#Around his lifted throat? v#Is not this mezzotint enough and surfeit v#For such prescience? v#Ah, did I dare v#Recall the pulsing life I gave, v#And fold him in the kindly grave!

v#Proud? v#Perhaps—could I but ever so faintly scan v#The broad horizon of a man v#Swept fair for his dominion— v#So hesitant and half-afraid v#I view this babe of sorrow!

Black Woman

v#Don’t knock at my door, little child, v##I cannot let you in. v#You know not what a world this is v##Of cruelty and sin. v#Wait in the still eternity v##Until I come to you, v#The world is cruel, cruel, child, v##I cannot let you in!

v#Don’t knock at my heart, little one, v##I cannot bear the pain v#Of turning deaf-ear to your call v##Time and time again! v#You do not know the monster men v##Inhabiting the earth, v#Be still, be still, my precious child, v##I must not give you birth!

"One of the Least of These, My Little One"

v#The infant eyes look out amazed upon the frowning earth, v#A stranger, in a land now strange, child of the mantled-birth;

v#Waxing, he wonders more and more; the scowling grows apace; v#A world, behind its barring doors, reviles his ebon face:

v#Yet from this maelstrom issues forth a God-like entity, v#That loves a world all loveless, and smiles on Calvary!

Shall I Say, "My Son, You're Branded?"

v#Shall I say, “My son, you’re branded in this country’s pageantry, v#By strange subtleties you’re tethered, and no forum sets you free?” v#Shall I mark the young lights fading through your soul-enchannelled eye, v#As the dusky pall of shadows screen the highway of your sky?

v#Or shall I, with love prophetic, bid you dauntlessly arise, v#Spurn the handicap that clogs you, taking what the world denies, v#Bid you storm the sullen fortress wrought by prejudice and wrong v#With a faith that shall not falter, in your heart and on your tongue!

My Boy

v#I hear you singing happily, v##My boy of tarnished mien, v#Lifting your limpid, trustful gaze v##In innocence serene.

v#A thousand javelins of pain v##Assault my heaving breast v#When I behold the storm of years v##That beat without your nest.

v#O sing, my lark, your matin song v##Of joyous rhapsody, v#Distil the sweetness of the hours v##In gladsome ecstasy.

v#For time awaits your buoyant flight v#Across the bar of years, v#Sing, sing your song, my bonny lark, v#Before it melts in tears!

Guardianship

v#That dusky child upon your knee v#Is breath of God’s eternity; v#Direct his vision to the height— v#Let naught obscure his royal right.

v#Although the highways to renown v#Are iron-barred by fortune’s frown, v#’Tis his to forge the master-key v#That wields the locks of destiny!

Utopia

v#God grant you wider vision, clearer skies, my son, v#With morning’s rosy kisses on your brow; v#May your wild yearnings know repose, v#And storm-clouds break to smiles v#As you sweep on with spreading wings v#Unto a waiting sunset!

Little Son

v#The very acme of my woe, v##The pivot of my pride, v#My consolation, and my hope v##Deferred, but not denied. v#The substance of my every dream, v##The riddle of my plight, v#The very world epitomized v##In turmoil and delight.

Benediction

v#Go forth, my son, v#Winged by my heart’s desire! v#Great reaches, yet unknown, v#Await v#For your possession. v#I may not, if I would, v#Retrace the way with you, v#My pilgrimage is through, v#But life is calling you! v#Fare high and far, my son, v#A new day has begun, v#Thy star-ways must be won!

Credo

v#I believe in the ultimate justice of Fate; v#That the races of men front the sun in their turn; v#That each soul holds the title to infinite wealth v#In fee to the will as it masters itself; v#That the heart of humanity sounds the same tone v#In impious jungle, or sky-kneeling fane. v#I believe that the key to the life-mystery v#Lies deeper than reason and further than death. v#I believe that the rhythmical conscience within v#Is guidance enough for the conduct of men.

Promise

v#Through the moil and the gloom they have issued v##To the steps of the upwinding hill, v#Where the sweet, dulcet pipes of tomorrow v##In their preluding rhapsodies trill.

v#With a thud comes a stir in the bosom, v##As there steals on the sight from afar, v#Through a break of a cloud’s coiling shadow v##The gleam of a bright morning star!

The Suppliant

v#Long have I beat with timid hands upon life’s leaden door, v#Praying the patient, futile prayer my fathers prayed before, v#Yet I remain without the close, unheeded and unheard, v#And never to my listening ear is borne the waited word.

v#Soft o’er the threshold of the years there comes this counsel cool:
v#The strong demand, contend, prevail; the beggar is a fool!

Hope

v#Frail children of sorrow, dethroned by a hue, v#The shadows are flecked by the rose sifting through, v#The world has its motion, all things pass away, v#No night is omnipotent, there must be day.

v#The oak tarries long in the depth of the seed, v#But swift is the season of nettle and weed, v#Abide yet awhile in the mellowing shade, v#And rise with the hour for which you were made.

v#The cycle of seasons, the tidals of man v#Revolve in the orb of an infinite plan. v#We move to the rhythm of ages long done, v#And each has his hour—to dwell in the sun!

Cosmopolite

v#Not wholly this or that, v#But wrought v#Of alien bloods am I, v#A product of the interplay v#Of traveled hearts. v#Estranged, yet not estranged, I stand v#All comprehending; v#From my estate v#I view earth’s frail dilemma; v#Scion of fused strength am I, v#All understanding, v#Nor this nor that v#Contains me.

Fusion

v#How deftly does the gardener blend v#This rose and that v#To bud a new creation, v#More gorgeous and more beautiful v#Than any parent portion, v#And so, v#I trace within my warring blood v#The tributary sources, v#They potently commingle v#And sweep v#With new-born forces!

Perspective

v#Some day v#I shall be glad that it was mine to be v#A dark fore-runner of a race burgeoning; v#I then shall know v#The secret of life’s Calvary, v#And bless the thorns v#That wound me!

When I Rise Up

v#When I rise above the earth, v#And look down on the things that fetter me, v#I beat my wings upon the air, v#Or tranquil lie, v#Surge after surge of potent strength v#Like incense comes to me v#When I rise up above the earth v#And look down upon the things that fetter me.

Faith

v#The faint lose faith v#When in the tomb their all is laid, v#And there returns v#No echoing of weal or woe. v#The strong hope on, v#They see the clods close over head, v#The grass grow green, v#No word is said, v#And yet— v#A little world within the world v#Are we, v#Daily our hearts’ high yearnings fade, v#Are buried! v#New ones are made,— v#Are crucified! v#And yet—

We Face the Future

v#The hour is big with sooth and sign, with errant men at war, v#While blood of alien, friend, and foe imbues the land afar, v#And we, with sable faces pent, move with the vanguard line, v#Shod with a faith that Springtime keeps, and all the stars opine.

Soldier

v#Though I should weep until the judgment, v#How would it serve— v#Brave men are fighting, women speed them, v#’Tis a day v#Of crucial conflict! v#My son, sometimes it seems I’d rather hold v#You safe beneath my heart v#Than send you forth! v#But lo! The sun is red and weaker children go! v#Though I should weep until the judgment, v#How would it serve! v#I’ll close my eyes and smile, O Son of Mine, v#Your cause is kingly! v#Step proud and confident, worthy your mother; v#Be firm and brave, O Son of Mine, be strong, v#For terror waxeth, v#Speed swift away, v#Though I should weep until the judgment . . .

Homing Braves

v#There’s music in the measured tread v#Of those returning from the dead v#Like scattered flowers from a plain v#So lately crimson, with the slain.

v#No more the sound of shuffled feet v#Shall mark the poltroon on the street, v#Nor shifting, sodden, downcast eye v#Reveal the man afraid to die.

v#They shall have paid full, utterly v#The price of peace across the sea, v#When, with uplifted glance, they come v#To claim a kindly welcome home.

v#Nor shall the old-time daedal sting v#Of prejudice, their manhood wing, v#Nor heights, nor depths, nor living streams v#Stand in the pathway of their dreams!

Taps

v#They are embosomed in the sod, v##In still and tranquil leisure, v#Their lives they’ve cast like trifles down, v##To serve their country’s pleasure.

v#Nor bugle call, nor mother’s voice, v##Nor moody mob’s unreason, v#Shall break their solace and repose v##Through swiftly changing season.

v#O graves of men who lived and died v##Afar from life’s high pleasures, v#Fold them in tenderly and warm v##With manifold fond measures.

Peace

v#Peace on a thousand hills and dales, v#Peace in the hearts of men v#While kindliness reclaims the soil v#Where bitterness has been.

v#The night of strife is drifting past, v#The storm of shell has ceased, v#Disrupted is the cordon fell, v#Sweet charity released.

v#Forth from the shadow, swift we come v#Wrought in the flame together, v#All men as one beneath the sun v#In brotherhood forever.

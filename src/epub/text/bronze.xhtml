To Mary Church Turrell—Lecturer

v#A pioneer, she blazed a trail of light v#Through murky shadows, with a lithesome tread v#Unto those forums, where Hope's beams are shed: v#Straight through the mighty cordon of the night, v#Rapt with a vision, soul-born, clear and bright, v#Leaving the South of frigid wrong, she sped v#Into the North, where hearts glow warm instead, v#A people's tragedy to there recite.

v#Hope's liquid pipings lift their tender lay, v#Morning is waking, flushed with rosy gleam, v#Night with its shadow winds with yesterday v#Adown the world-way as an inky stream, v#Seed time and harvest deftly interplay, v#And Life's fruition is its vital dream!

To May Howard Jackson—Sculptor

v#You saw the vision in the face of clay, v#And fixed it through the magic of a hand v#Obedient unto the will's command, v#In forms impervious to Time's decay: v#Historian of bloods that interplay v#Confusedly within a cryptic land, v#You've chiseled, and your work of art shall stand v#To gem the archives of a better day.

v#Alone, far from the touch of kindred mind, v#You've mounted with a grim, determined zeal, v#Despite environment austere, unkind, v#Or frozen-fingers clenched to your appeal, v#You've held the ardor of your first ideal, v#Robed in a queenly majesty, resigned.

To the Memory of Inez Milholland

v#Folded in silent veils of sleep, v##You calmly rest, v#For God hath spoken, should we weep? v##He knoweth best.

v#But rather let us garner still v##While yet we may, v#And meet you in His Holy Hill v##On that Great Day!

To Atlanta University—Its Founders and Teachers

v#Pass down the aisle of buried years to-night, v#And stand uncovered in that holy place v#Where noble structures lift their hallowed height v#Beneath a bending Heaven's chaste embrace, v#The fruit of those who scorned the path of ease, v#To buckle on the armaments of care v#Like to the Son of Man Himself, were these v#Who gave themselves for brother men—less fair.

v#Before the blinding footlights of to-day v#We man our parts within Life's tragic play, v#Full mindful of the earnest love and care v#That keeps eternal watch and vigil there; v#Nor do they need fair monuments and scrolls— v#Their memories are deathless in our souls.

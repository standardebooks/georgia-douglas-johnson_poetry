Footsteps

v#Passing ever, early, late, v#No fond footsteps seek my gate, v#But down the winding road they wend v#To some other journey’s end.

v#Yet,—I would not have them wait v#Here within my guarded gate, v#Certain footsteps I shall know, v#And for them I listen low!

Oh Night of Love

v#Oh night of love, your rapt ecstatic hours v#Were mine, the languor of their pale perfume v#Pervades me, kisses in a fountain-fire, v#Surround me,—fetter and consume.

v#Oh night of love, your groves of strange content v#Project a thralldom over coming days; v#Exalted, derelict, and blind I wend v#Unmindfully along Life’s misty ways.

Autumn

v#Believe me–when I say v#That love like yours, at this belated hour, v#Overwhelms me,— v#Stills the fount of thought! v#I move as one new-born– v#And strange to swift transitions v#As from my prison door v#I gaze v#Into a blinding sunlight!

Thralldom

v#Your voice keeps ringing down the day v###In accents soft and mild, v###With which you have beguiled v###And wooed me as a child.

v#Your presence bounds my every way v###And thrills me in its fold v###With phantom hands that hold v###Like cherished chains of gold.

Separation

v#Within your pulsing day v#There must be little space v#For visions of my face v#To lure your thoughts away.

v#Yet, I would have it so, v#To bear alone the pain v#That saddens love’s refrain. v#Pray God you never know!

Love's Miracle

v#So like a boundless, soundless sea v#The miracle of love to me, v#With all the world a rosy dream v#Sailing upon a silver stream, v#While I, a fairy in mid-air, v#Am dancing, dancing everywhere.

v#Hark! do you hear the thunder peal? v#I care not what it would reveal, v#Tomorrow will be yesterday v#When I am shivering and gray:

v#I will not heed the prompter's ring v#Let others answer, I shall sing v#And dance the merrier—away! v#I'll live and live and live—today!

Proving

v#Were you a leper bathed in wounds v##And by the world denied, v#I’d share your fatal exile v##As a privilege, with pride.

v#You are the very sun, the moon, v##The starlight of my soul, v#The sounding motif of my heart v##Its impetus and goal!

Interim

v#The days lie dark between our jeweled meetings v#Like wintry burials.

v#My heart bows low before the cheerless hearth v#Until your voice rings through the gloom v#And bids me v#Wake! v#And live!

Good-Bye

v#Let’s say “Good-bye” v#Nor wait Love’s latest breath v#Poised now so lightly on the wing of Death, v#While yet within our eyes one fervent gleam v#Remains to hallow this, a passing dream: v#Yes, yes “Good-bye,” v#For it is best to part v#While Love’s low light still burns v#Within the heart!

A Paradox

v#I know you love me better cold v#Strange as the pyramids of old v#Responselessly. v#But I am frail, and spent and weak v#With surging torrents that bespeak v#A living fire. v#So, like a veil, my poor disguise v#Is draped to save me from your eyes' v#Deep challenges. v#Fain would I fling this robe aside v#And from you, in your bosom hide v#Eternally. v#Alas! you love me better cold v#Like frozen pyramids of old v#Unyieldingly?

How My Heart Sinks

v#How my heart sinks when I behold the sad reflection of my face, v#A wan and wistful wound, with oh, such meagre grace; v#How can you hold me dear withal and conjure charms withdrawn. v#Or does the Autumn twilight hold a charm unknown to dawn?

v#Hold! Do not speak! some day perchance, I'll read the message dire v#Within the ashes of the flame, the aftermath of fire, v#Ere then perhaps I shall have found the highways of the soul v#Where one may read uncrucified, the blood-words of the scroll. v#Till then, uphold illusion's veil before my gaze the while v#That I may gather strength to fuse from agony, a smile!

To Time

v#Day by day the threads of white v#Muliply, Oh! hour-glass! v#How passing swift your bright sands pass, v#Fain would I hold you, v#Linger, bide v#Until these surges shall subside, v#That sweep me forward unto bliss, v#Oh! charging sun, I bid you rest, v#Break not your arrow in my breast!

Welt

v#Would I might mend the fabric of my youth v#Which daily flaunts its tatters to my eyes, v#Would I might compromise awhile with truth v#Until love's moon, now waxing, wanes and dies.

v#For I would go a further while with you v#And draing this Cup of Joy so passing fair, v#Which meets my parching lips like cooling dew v#'Ere time has brushed cold fingers through my hair.

Review

v#I fear my power impotent v#To hold you leal and full content, v#Some hapless look or word perchance v#Dispels the glamour of romance; v#I tremble lest some stranger fair v#Arrest you,—cause you to compare v#The meagre charms which I possess v#With some resplendent loveliness.

v#How far removed from Youth's command v#The trembling sceptre in my hand, v#As miserly within the glass v#I mark Love's fleeting hours pass.

Illusion

v#Oh! for the veils of my far-away youth, v#Shielding my heart from the blaze of the truth; v#Why did I stray from their foldings and grow v#Into the sadness that follows—to know.

v#Impotent atom with desolate gaze v#Treading Life's treacherous, intricate maze— v#Oh for the veils, for the vails of my youth v#Shielding my heart from the blaze of the truth!

Parody

v#You came, v#The tapestries of love v#Were shining in the sun, v#My wishes settled down content v#About you as you stood. v#I looked into your cryptic eyes v#And thought I understood; v#But no,— v#The splendor of your gaudy robe v#Grew dimmer day by day, v#I wondered, v#Searched within my soul to seize the mystery. v#The answer staggered me, v#Aghast, v#Like one at bay, v#I gazed with open eyes of thought upon you, v#God! 'twas true— v#A mockery, a parody, v#Had come to me—in you!

Delusion

v#You gave me your hand, v#I held it to be v#The last word, the dear word, v#The soul's entity; v#I cherished it, treasured it, v#Only to find v#I held but a gauntlet— v#That I had been blind!

Sunset

v#And now— v#As one who closes up the house and goes uncaring where v#He may forget the scenes of home 'mid foreign climes and air, v#I bar the chamber of my heart and seal the past within v#To wander down the city's road amid the whirr and din. v#The long years seem impassable, the morning has no smile, v#With naught behind these barring doors and nothing else worth while, v#Like some lone pilgrim without hope, I stumble on my way, v#Who lifts no futile plea for sun, but asks for clouds less grey.

Finis

v#I looked death calmly in the face v#And placed my hand within his hand v#And said: v#"Come, come, let us away v#For I have lost the magic key v#Opening the portals of desire— v#My wishes cumber in the dust, v#And life is stagnant v#in v#my v#heart!

Ivy

v#I am a woman v#Which means v#I am insufficient v#I need— v#Something to hold me v#Or perhaps uphold. v#I am a woman.

Joy

v#There's nothing certain, nothing sure v#Save sorrow. Fragile happiness v#Was never fashioned to endure; v#For joy repels the perfect claim v#And answers to no certain name; v#How furtively we scan the mist v#Perchance amid the gloom to find v#Some moments rare and rapture-kist.

One Day

v#Good-bye dear day of sunshine, rain v##In flooding torrents pours v#Its liquid footsteps on my roof, v##Its fingers on my doors.

v#While I sit tranquilly within v##And tell my beads of joy, v#Holding a peace within my heart v##Which nothing can destroy.

Attar

v#Fire—tears— v#And the torture-chamber, v#With the last maddening turn of the screw— v#Only thus v#Is one precious drop distilled v#Of the attar of rose v#Of the heart.

Youth's Progeny

v#Oh the sad little dreams of the dim yesteryear v#Lying cold, still and stark in the dust of their bier, v#How the heart hurries back, all the long weary way, v#Just to bid them good-night at the close of the day.

I Wonder

v#I wonder— v####as I see them pass unheeded down the way, v#(The women who were once beloved, imperious and gay) v#Holding with frail, pale hands the cup v#Of Life's discarded wine v#If memories v#Are bliss enough v#To make the dregs—divine!

Values

v#All the pretty baubles spread v#Are not the answer to my need, v#These tinseled trappings but beguile v#This journeying, while deep within v#A want unspeakable resides, v#That throbs and throbs unceasingly,— v#So hungering,—no banquet spread v#Can tempt it, and no golden wine v#Make it forget: I balance it— v#The world flies upward in the scale! v#Always, unsoothed, unquieted, v#It aches and aches across the days v#And sears the nights that sum my life.

Armageddon

v#In the silence and the dark v#I fought with dragons; v#I was battered, beaten sore v#But rose again; v#On my knees I fought still rising v#In my pain: v#In the dark I fought with dragons. v#Weary tears v#Cease your flowing, v#Even now, the dawn appears!

v#Mute-lipped— v#unquestioning grim-visaged Fate, v#I cleave the shadows toward the Western Gate; v#And yet— v#my lagging heart still holds v#Mute-arms outstretched v#Unto earth's gleaming folds.

v#Who knows? v#perhaps Hope’s blossoms spray v#In lush profusion v#O'er the edge of day!

Treasure

v#What matters though love's dream shall pass, v#Since from the throbbing hour-glass v#One golden-throated moment prest v#Its attared incense to my breast.

v#Since I have known the purple gleam v#That lifts above me—can I deem v#The way unlighted—when I go v#Encircled by love's afterglow?

Retrospection

v#After all— v#mine is the joy v#Which naught can lessen or destroy. v#For love has led my flying feet v#Where immortelles are springing sweet, v#And everlasting skies of gold v#Are memories, when earth is cold v#And though our future paths should lie v#Estranged, as star-ways, through the sky, v#I shall not look reproof, nor find v#Within this pass a charge unkind, v#And lightly sorrow shall be met v#For I can never know regret.

v#Again it is the vibrant May, v##The bursting buds, the leafing trees, v##The fragrant, undulating breeze, v##Call to my heart in subtlest way: v##Come! come! it is a holiday.

v#The streamlet with unending song, v##Beneath its silver veil of mist v##Seems flowing, flowing, to some tryst, v##While I—with inner surges strong, v##Find incomplete the day, and long.

v#I know my love is seeking me v#As Time must seek Eternity, v#When nights are very still I hear v#His footsteps, coming, coming near!

v#Love calls me tonight v#In the beat of the rain v#Through the cold little drops v#On my bare window-pane; v#Calls and calls through the dark v#Like a whispered refrain v#Tapping soft on my heart v#Through the bare window pane.

v#Love me! v#The day is fleet v#And I ... v#Am far too passionate v#To die!
